# North Cascades National Park

The North Cascades are a rugged, mountainous region in northern Washington State, forming the northernmost section of the Cascade Range.
It features sharp granite spires, glaciers, deep valleys, and dense conifer forests.

In geology, spires are tall, narrow, pointed rock formations usually the result of erosion and glacial carving acting on hard bedrock, like granite.

## Washington Pass Vista Point

Washington Pass Vista Point, also known as Washington Pass Overlook, sits at an elevation of about 5,477 ft (1,669 m) along State Route 20 in the North Cascades mountains. 

It provides panoramic views of legendary peaks like `Liberty Bell Mountain`, the `Early Winters Spires`, the `Vasiliki Ridge`, as well as the winding of the Highway 20 which crosses the National Park.

## Frenchman Coulee

The Frenchman Coulee is a dramatic basalt coulee in central Washington.
It was carved by ancient ice age floods that sculpted channels through the Columbia River basalt.

In geology, a coulee is usually the product of rapid, catastrophic flooding.
It looks like a gorge or canyon, but they are typically formed by erosion from water over time, and not flooding like a coulee. 
Aslo the difference between canyon and gorge is that the last is essentially a narrower, steeper version of a canyon.

You can access the Frenchman Coulee via the Exit 143 on the I‑90.

## Seattle Wheel

The Seattle Great Wheel is a large Ferris wheel located on Pier 57 along the Seattle waterfront in Washington, USA.
The Seattle Great Wheel is permanently installed on a pier that extends over Elliott Bay.
It gives riders panoramic views of downtown Seattle, the Puget Sound, and — on clear days — Mount Rainier and the Olympic Mountains.
