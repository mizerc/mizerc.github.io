# Project Management

Project management is a structured approach to planning, organizing, and directing resources to achieve specific goals within defined constraints like time, budget, and scope.
In software development, the goal normally is to deliver the software changes to achieve product or engineering requirements.

# Methodology

The methodology is a specific way of managing the project.
Examples of methodologies are Agile, Kanban, and Scrum.

# Retrospective

A retrospective is a meeting held to review and reflect on a project or sprint in an Agile environment, with the goal of identifying improvements for future work.

# Deployment Techniques

Deployment techniques are the process of deploying the software changes to the production environment.

## Controlled Rollout

The controlled rollout is a deployment technique where the changes are deployed to a small subset of the users.

## Feature Flags

Feature flags are a deployment technique where the changes are deployed to a small subset of the users without the need to deploy a new version of the software.
Flags can also reduce the risk of deploying the changes to the production environment.
As well as allowing to test the changes in a production environment before rolling out to all the users.

# Risk Management

Risk is the possibility of something bad happening.  

## Mitigation

Mitigation is the process of reducing the likelihood or impact of a risk.

## Rollback

Rollback is the process of undoing the changes made to a project.

# Summary

## Scope Management

Defining the project boundaries, including deliverables, timelines, and resources.

## Time Management

Creating schedules, setting milestones, and tracking progress (Gantt charts, epic burndown charts).

## Resource Management

Coordinating developers, testers, designers, and stakeholders (resource allocation, communication).

## Risk Management

Identifying, analyzing, and mitigating potential issues that could derail the project.

## Quality Management

Ensuring the software meets standards and customer expectations (via testing, reviews).
