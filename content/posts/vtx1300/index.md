## Carburator

### Main Jet

The 2004 Honda VTX 1300C is equipped with a Keihin 38mm Constant Velocity (CV) carburetor.
This carburetor utilizes a vacuum-operated slide mechanism.
The stock main jet size for this model is #195 (1.95mm) with slot-head design and M5x0.8 thread

The main jet is a calibrated orifice (brass nozzle) inside a carburetor that meters the fuel flow at mid-to-full throttle.
From 3/4 to WOT (Wide-Open Throttle).
It's part of the main fuel circuit and works with the needle and needle jet.

As throttle opens, engine vacuum draws air through the venturi.
The pressure drop at the venturi pulls fuel through the main jet.
The jet restricts fuel flow to a specific rate based on its orifice size.
Fuel then mixes with air and enters the combustion chamber.

### Pilot Jet

Aka A/F screw!
Controls idle to ~1/4 throttle.
The pilot or idle jet.
The pilot jet, and pilot screw control fuel delivery from idle to approximately 1/8 throttle.

### Air screw (on the air filter side)

Loose = More air
Tight = Less air

### Fuel screw (on the engine side)

Loosing = More gas (rich mix)
Tight = Less gass (lean mix)
Note that most screws and bolts have right-handed threads, meaning turning them clockwise (righty) tightens them, and turning them counterclockwise (lefty) loosens them.

### Keihin Carbutetors

Keihin carburetors are a family of mechanical fuel-mixing devices (carburetors) made by Keihin Corporation (Japan), widely used in motorcycles, ATVs, and powersports engines.

### Jetting

Jetting is the process of adjusting fuel delivery in a carburetor to match engine needs.
