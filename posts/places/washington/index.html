Tulip Festival#

Around April, when tulips are in full bloom, several farms in Washington open their fields to the public. One of the most popular destinations is located north of Seattle in Skagit Valley, especially near the town of Mount Vernon. The area is home to large tulip farms such as Roozengaarde and Tulip Town, where visitors can walk among vast, colorful rows of tulips stretching across acres.

These farms are part of the annual Skagit Valley Tulip Festival, which attracts thousands of visitors and photographers every spring. Check available gardens at https://tulipfestival.org/garden-tickets/.

Frenchman Coulee#

The Frenchman Coulee is a dramatic basalt coulee in central Washington. It was carved by ancient ice age floods that sculpted channels through the Columbia River basalt.

In geology, a coulee is usually the product of rapid, catastrophic flooding. It looks like a gorge or canyon, but they are typically formed by erosion from water over time, and not flooding like a coulee. Aslo the difference between canyon and gorge is that the last is essentially a narrower, steeper version of a canyon.

Cherry Blossoms#

Around March to April, cherry blossoms (genus Prunus, typically Prunus serrulata) begin to bloom across various parts of the Pacific Northwest. In Seattle, one of the best spots to see them is the University of Washington campus, especially in the Quad, where mature Yoshino/Sakura cherry trees create a dense canopy of soft white flowers.

North Cascades National Park

The North Cascades are a rugged, mountainous region in northern Washington State, forming the northernmost section of the Cascade Range. It features sharp granite spires, glaciers, deep valleys, and dense conifer forests. In geology, spires are tall, narrow, pointed rock formations usually the result of erosion and glacial carving acting on hard bedrock, like granite. Location North Cascades National Park is located in northern Washington state, in the United States.
